Prepare to Launch


Going into space for the first time can be quite the disorientating task, so it is very important to take the entire process extremely seriously and listen carefully to our experienced guides so that your first experience in space can be enjoyable and successful. At Space Tourism, we take pride in providing a smooth and safe launch experience for all of our travelers. We understand that going into space can be an intimidating experience, but with the help of our guides and a bit of preparation, it is possible to make the journey as enjoyable and stress-free as possible. All travelers will be required to keep their holsters on at all times until they are instructed to remove them, after that, they’ll be able to fully take in the stars.

Recovery


Upon returning to Earth, travelers will need to learn how to readjust to Earth’s gravity and gain a sense of their own weight again. The process of readjustment can be a difficult one; the traveler will need to re-learn how to move their body and regulate their balance with gravity. Because of this, the final three days of your journey will include a complimentary training regime to ensure your transition to the blue planet will be as if you never left. Through physical activities such as swimming and weightlifting, travelers will be able to slowly regain the ability to move their bodies with normal gravity. For their safety, our travelers will be instructed to ease up for roughly a week after returning to their regular lives.






Our Safety Promise

Our ships use the best technology to keep our travelers safe. With numerous safety plans set for any kind of incident. In case of emergency all of our ships are prepared with evacuation pods that will return you to the nearest space station for recovery.
